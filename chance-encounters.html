BUMBLE: CHANCE ENCOUNTERS

Creating organic interactions between BFF & BIZZ users through geolocation.

LOGISTICS
1 Month, 1 Person

I was tasked to create a new geolocation feature for Bumble, but make it with the Google Material Design system.

BUSINESS GOAL
$$$

Bumble wanted to convert existing, non-paying users of BFF & BIZZ to subscribe to a new geolocation feature.

SOLUTION

Embrace randomness, but deepen the connection with a collaborative game.

My design focused on creating a design intervention that facilitates organic connections with people you pass by, enhancing the user experience by bridging digital interactions with real-world encounters.

A fellow BFF / BIZZ user has entered your proximity.

You have 60 seconds to view their profile -- or you may never cross paths again!

Let's play a social card-collecting game.

USER A

You just matched with B, and you want to try this new feature.

You receive a hint of B's location. Find each other to know the correct answer for the multiple choice!

USER B

You can preview the uniquely generated Pollen Card, which is the correct answer.

You look around for someone who looks like your match -- there!

Remember every chance encounter.

You have found each other while playing the game. Maybe one of you have to quickly say goodbye, but no worries.

The Pollen Card lives in your conversation as a memory of your first encounter.

REFLECTION

Subvert and surrender.

My professor said we should watch 90s romance movies for inspiration. I didn't watch any, because I hate corny movies, but I understood the intention to create a romanticized version of reality, which is something that dating apps lack.

The algorithms are mediocre, and the interactions are forced.

Chance Encounters exist, because I wanted to meet someone by chance and have a memorable experience. I want subvert the algorithm and surrender myself to the randomness of the universe.
